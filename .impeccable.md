## Design Context

### Users
SimSuite is for Sims players, mod collectors, and creators who manage a local mod library on desktop. They open the app in two main moods: a quick "is everything okay before I play?" check, or a longer cleanup session where they want calm, reliable information without feeling buried under tools.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel cosy, casual, and non-confusing. It should feel warm and personal rather than corporate, but still trustworthy enough for file management. The emotional goal is calm confidence: the app should feel easy to approach, easy to read, and never frantic.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is a flat, desktop-first workspace with softer atmosphere and more sophistication than a plain utility app. The best reference mood is closer to a browser home tab or a clean ChatGPT home page than a dense dashboard. Home surfaces should avoid looking like a wall of cards. Use color themes, soft ambient vector layers, and subtle motion instead of wallpapers, busy illustrations, or animated GIF backgrounds. Keep navigation in the sidebar and keep the main home surface focused on glanceable system health, not a menu of shortcuts.

### Design Principles
1. Show less, mean more.
Only the most useful information should stay visible by default. Supporting detail should appear through drawers, sheets, expanders, or secondary states.

2. Calm first, power second.
Every screen should feel easy to scan at a glance, even when deeper tools exist underneath. Advanced controls should stay available, but not loud.

3. Personal, not decorative.
Personalization should come from theme, layout choice, module visibility, and density control, not from loud wallpaper systems.

4. Stable desktop rhythm.
Design for a proper desktop app: strong alignment, restrained motion, minimal page scrolling, and clear fixed work areas.

5. Gentle clarity across all modes.
Casual, Seasoned, and Creator should share the same structure, while changing how much detail is shown, how much copy is visible, and which modules are open by default.
